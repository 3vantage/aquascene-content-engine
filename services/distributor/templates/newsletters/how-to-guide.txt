STEP 1: Tank Preparation
--------------------------------------------------
Clean your aquarium thoroughly with warm water. Avoid using any detergents or chemicals. Rinse until there's no residue left and dry with a clean, lint-free cloth.

üí° Tip: Position your tank in its final location before adding substrate - it will be much heavier afterward!

STEP 2: Substrate Layout
--------------------------------------------------
Create a sloping substrate bed, higher in the back (6-8cm) and lower in the front (3-4cm). This creates depth and helps with the overall composition.

‚ö†Ô∏è Important: Don't rinse aqua soil - it will break down and cloud your water.

STEP 3: Hardscape Placement
--------------------------------------------------
Arrange your rocks and driftwood following the rule of thirds. Create focal points and ensure the hardscape looks natural from the front viewing angle.

üí° Tip: Take photos from different angles to check your composition before proceeding.

STEP 4: Initial Water Fill
--------------------------------------------------
Slowly add water using a plate or bowl to diffuse the flow. Fill to about 50% capacity to make planting easier while preventing substrate disturbance.

Problem: Cloudy Water After Setup
Solution: This is normal with aqua soil. Run your filter and the water will clear in 24-48 hours. Avoid large water changes initially.

Problem: Plants Floating or Uprooting
Solution: Plant deeper and use tweezers to firmly anchor roots. Some floating is normal in the first week.

Problem: Algae Growth in First Month
Solution: Reduce lighting to 6 hours daily and increase CO2. This is part of the natural cycling process.
